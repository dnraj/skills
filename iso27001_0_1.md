# ISO 27001 Managed Services Implementation

## When to Use This Skill

Use this skill when the user asks to build, assess, plan, or operationalize ISO/IEC 27001 compliance for a managed services organization, including MSPs, MSSPs, IT outsourcing providers, cloud operations providers, managed infrastructure teams, help desk providers, backup providers, endpoint management providers, and organizations that administer customer environments.

Use this skill for:

- Building an Information Security Management System from scratch.
- Creating an ISO 27001 implementation roadmap.
- Creating a managed-services-specific control matrix.
- Drafting an ISMS scope statement.
- Building a risk register, risk treatment plan, or Statement of Applicability.
- Creating evidence request lists for certification readiness.
- Preparing Stage 1 and Stage 2 audit readiness materials.
- Mapping managed services operations to ISO/IEC 27001:2022 clauses and Annex A controls.
- Defining controls for privileged access, customer environment access, RMM tools, ticketing systems, cloud operations, backups, monitoring, incident response, change management, patching, and supplier management.

Default to ISO/IEC 27001:2022 unless the user specifies another edition.

## Core Approach

Treat ISO 27001 as an operating management system, not a document checklist. Build the ISMS around the managed services business model, customer commitments, risk exposure, service delivery workflows, privileged access, critical service tooling, and evidence that demonstrates control performance.

Always separate:

- **Clauses 4-10**: Mandatory ISMS governance requirements.
- **Annex A controls**: Risk treatment controls selected through the Statement of Applicability.
- **Design evidence**: Policies, procedures, standards, roles, diagrams, scope, methodology.
- **Implementation evidence**: Tool configurations, inventories, workflows, system settings.
- **Operating effectiveness evidence**: Completed reviews, tickets, logs, approvals, test results, meeting minutes, exception records, and corrective actions.

Do not guarantee certification. Frame outputs as compliance support, audit preparation, and implementation guidance.

## Managed Services Context to Capture

When material, identify or ask for:

- Services provided: help desk, RMM, endpoint management, monitoring, backup, cloud operations, SOC, MSSP, network administration, patch management, or infrastructure operations.
- Customer environment access model: direct admin access, delegated access, VPN, RMM, PAM, cloud tenant access, break-glass access, or customer-approved sessions.
- In-scope systems: identity provider, RMM, PSA/ticketing, documentation platform, password vault, SIEM, EDR, backup platform, cloud consoles, monitoring tools, endpoint tools, MDM, HRIS, code repositories, and collaboration systems.
- Data types: customer data, credentials, configuration data, logs, tickets, screenshots, device telemetry, backup data, personal data, and confidential business data.
- Locations and workforce: offices, remote workers, contractors, offshore teams, subcontractors.
- Certification boundary: entire organization, managed services division, specific service line, or specific geography.

If details are missing, proceed with reasonable assumptions and list them clearly.

## Standard Workflow

### Establish scope and governance

Create or validate:

- ISMS scope statement.
- Interested parties and requirements register.
- Information security policy.
- Security roles and responsibilities.
- ISMS governance forum and cadence.
- Information security objectives.
- In-scope asset, service, customer environment, and vendor inventories.

### Build the risk foundation

Create:

- Risk assessment methodology.
- Risk register.
- Risk treatment plan.
- Risk acceptance process.
- Statement of Applicability.

Managed-services-specific risk scenarios should include:

- Unauthorized access to customer environments.
- Privileged account misuse.
- RMM or remote access platform compromise.
- Customer data exposure through tickets, documentation, screenshots, or logs.
- Missed patching or vulnerability remediation obligations.
- Failed backup or restore.
- Inadequate customer segregation.
- Incomplete offboarding of engineers or contractors.
- Supplier platform compromise.
- Security incident escalation failure.
- Unauthorized or unapproved customer-impacting changes.

### Design core policy set

At minimum, include:

- Information Security Policy.
- Access Control Policy.
- Privileged Access Management Procedure.
- Customer Environment Access Procedure.
- Asset Management Procedure.
- Risk Management Procedure.
- Data Classification and Handling Policy.
- Incident Response Plan.
- Change Management Procedure.
- Vulnerability and Patch Management Procedure.
- Backup and Recovery Procedure.
- Supplier Management Procedure.
- Business Continuity Plan.
- Logging and Monitoring Procedure.
- Secure Configuration Standard.
- HR Security Procedure.
- Acceptable Use Policy.

### Implement priority controls

Prioritize controls that address the highest managed services risks:

- MFA for internal systems and customer administration.
- Named accounts and least privilege for customer access.
- Privileged access management or password vault controls.
- Quarterly user and privileged access reviews.
- Joiner, mover, leaver workflow.
- RMM and remote access hardening.
- Ticketing and documentation access control.
- Change approval and emergency change review.
- Vulnerability scanning, patch tracking, and remediation SLAs.
- Backup monitoring and restore testing.
- Security logging and alert review.
- Incident response escalation and customer notification.
- Vendor risk management for critical platforms.
- Security awareness training.
- Internal audit, management review, corrective action, and continual improvement.

### Prepare audit evidence

For every control, define:

- Control objective.
- Control activity.
- Owner.
- Frequency.
- Systems and tools.
- Design evidence.
- Implementation evidence.
- Operating evidence.
- Exception handling.
- Testing approach.

Auditors will usually expect several months of operating evidence for Stage 2, depending on certification body expectations and audit scope.

## Roadmap Template

Use this structure for an ISO 27001 implementation roadmap:

| Phase | Timeline | Objective | Key activities | Deliverables | Owners | Audit output |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Foundation | Weeks 1-2 | Define ISMS scope and governance | Scope, stakeholders, policy, roles, objectives | Scope statement, policy, RACI | Executive sponsor, ISMS owner | Clause 4-6 evidence |
| Risk and SoA | Weeks 3-5 | Build risk-based control framework | Risk methodology, risk assessment, risk treatment, SoA | Risk register, treatment plan, SoA | ISMS owner, security, operations | Risk and control selection evidence |
| Control Design | Weeks 6-8 | Document and configure controls | Policies, procedures, inventories, workflows | Policy set, asset register, vendor register | Control owners | Design and implementation evidence |
| Control Operation | Weeks 9-16 | Run controls and collect evidence | Reviews, tickets, logging, training, backup tests | Operating evidence library | Control owners | Stage 2 evidence |
| Assurance | Weeks 17-20 | Validate readiness | Internal audit, management review, corrective actions | Internal audit report, management review minutes, CAPA tracker | Independent auditor, leadership | Clause 9-10 evidence |
| Certification Readiness | Weeks 21-24 | Prepare for external audit | Evidence QA, pre-assessment, remediation closure | Stage 1 and Stage 2 audit package | ISMS owner | Certification audit package |

Adjust timelines based on company maturity, number of systems, certification scope, and available resources.

## Control Matrix Template

Use this structure when creating a managed services control matrix:

| Control ID | ISO reference | Control area | Managed services control activity | Owner | Frequency | Evidence | Evidence type | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ISO-MS-001 | Clause 4.3 | ISMS scope | Define and approve scope covering managed services, customer access, tooling, people, and locations | Executive sponsor / ISMS owner | Annual and upon major change | Scope statement, in-scope system list | Design | High |
| ISO-MS-002 | Annex A access controls | Privileged access | Review privileged access to customer and internal admin systems | IT/Security | Quarterly | Access review export, sign-off, remediation tickets | Operating effectiveness | Critical |

Control IDs should be unique and stable. Use prefixes such as `ISO-MS-001`.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing outputs:

- Confirm the ISMS scope and assumptions are clear.
- Distinguish ISO clauses from Annex A controls.
- Tie controls to managed services risk scenarios.
- Include control owners and frequencies.
- Include evidence that auditors can inspect.
- Include both Stage 1 documentation and Stage 2 operating evidence.
- Avoid generic control language without testable procedures.
- Include customer environment access, RMM/remote access, ticketing, vendor platforms, backups, logging, incident response, change management, and vulnerability management.
- State that certification outcomes depend on auditor assessment and actual implementation.
